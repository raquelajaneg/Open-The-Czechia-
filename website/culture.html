Czech Culture & Heritage

Discover the rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant traditions of the Czech Republic

Places You Can't Miss in Prague

Church of Our Lady before Týn

Dominating the Old Town Square with its iconic twin Gothic spires, the Church of Our Lady before Týn (commonly called Týn Church) is one of Prague's most recognizable landmarks. This stunning 14th-century church has witnessed centuries of history, serving as a symbol of the city's resilience and spiritual heritage.

Historical Significance

The church played a crucial role during the Hussite Wars and was the main church of the Hussite movement. Its 80-meter high towers offer one of the best views of Prague's historic center.

Inside, you'll find a breathtaking Baroque altar, the tomb of Danish astronomer Tycho Brahe, and beautiful Gothic vaulting. The church's asymmetrical towers (one slightly larger than the other) add to its unique character and have inspired artists and photographers for generations.

More Cultural Gems

Prague Castle

The largest ancient castle complex in the world, spanning over 70,000 m². A UNESCO World Heritage site that has been the seat of Czech kings, emperors, and presidents for over a thousand years.

Hradčany, Prague 1

Charles Bridge

This historic bridge connects the Old Town with Lesser Town, adorned with 30 Baroque statues of saints. Built in 1357, it's one of Prague's most romantic and photographed spots.

Between Old Town & Malá Strana

Jewish Quarter

Josefov is one of Europe's best-preserved Jewish quarters, featuring the Old Jewish Cemetery, several synagogues, and the Jewish Museum that tells the story of Prague's Jewish community.

Josefov, Prague 1

Cultural Insights

Traditional Festivals

Experience Prague's vibrant cultural scene through events like the Prague Spring International Music Festival, Christmas Markets, and Easter Markets in the Old Town Square.

Culinary Heritage

Try traditional Czech dishes like svíčková (beef with cream sauce), knedlíky (dumplings), and trdelník (chimney cake) while exploring the city's historic pubs and restaurants.

Musical Legacy

Prague is known as the "City of Music." Visit venues where Mozart, Dvořák, and Smetana performed, and attend classical concerts in historic churches and palaces.
